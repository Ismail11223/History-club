The most famous excavation and mound about Thracians

1.Beglintash

Beglintash is famous with Belantash or Beluntash names.There are suggestions that is cult object dating from 5 B.C.Translated from Turkish, it means the White Stone. It is a small rocky plateau bearing traces of human activity. In length, the rock platform reaches 300 meters, with an altitude of over 1200 meters. Belintash is located 30 km southeast of Asenovgrad. Other theories say that there was an ancient Thracian sanctuary dedicated to the god Sabazius, built and managed by the Thracian tribe of Besi. In the highest part of the rock are carved circular openings, niches, gutters and steps, which according to some researchers form a map of the starry sky.


2. Ruskina Dupka Cave

Ruskina Dupka Cave is located near the village of Zhelezovo in Burgas, on the north side of the medieval fortress of Rusokastro. According to archaeologists, there is evidence that the place was a sanctuary as early as the time of the Thracians. It is believed that the hill represents a huge Thracian sanctuary. The length of the cave is 17 m, and its height is 4 m. From the church on the hill, a marked path descends to the hole itself, and it can also be reached by road.


3.The early Thracian tomb

The early Thracian tomb is located near the village of Brodilovo, Burgas region. According to archaeologists, it dates back to the 4th century BC. It was discovered in 2008 and is believed to have belonged to a wealthy dynasty that controlled the ore in Strandja. The tomb was extremely expensive for its time.


4.The Thracian tomb Kuhata Mogila

It is located 100 m from the road to Pomorie and is dated to the 2nd-3rd century. It is a combination of a typical Thracian dome tomb and a Roman mausoleum. Its height is 8 m and it has a diameter of 60 m. It is open for visits and has a tour guide.


5. Thracian tomb in Pomorie

The site is unique for the Balkan Peninsula. It dates back to the end of the 3rd and the beginning of the 4th century AD and is defined as the latest representative of the domed Thracian tombs for the territory of Bulgaria, and it is also the largest Thracian tomb in our country.

6. The ancient dome tomb

The ancient dome tomb is located about 1 km from the city of Pomorie, (Burgas Region), 300 m from the Pomorie - Burgas highway. The tomb dates back to the 2nd - 3rd century. The accepted scientific hypothesis is that the structure served as a mausoleum (heroon) of a rich Anchial Thracian family, in which religious rituals dedicated to the cult of the sun were performed. The ancient domed tomb is the largest, publicly discovered in Bulgaria and the only one of its kind on the Balkan Peninsula. It is a combination of a typical Thracian dome tomb and a Roman mausoleum (heroon) under the embankment of a burial mound.


7. Aquae Calidae

Aquae Calidae (in Latin: Aquae Calidae, in translation at the hot waters) is a historical settlement on the territory of the city of Burgas, in the lands of the Vetren and Banevo districts. In the Middle Ages, it was mentioned under the names Therma and Thermopolis. It has been proven that the mineral baths were visited by Philip II of Macedon, the Roman emperors Justinian I and Constantine IV Pogonatus, the Byzantine empress Ino Anastasia, the Bulgarian Khan Tervel, and Sultan Suleiman the Magnificent. Today, the famous Burgas mineral baths are located on its territory.


8.Discovery

At the beginning of the summer of 2008, archaeologists from the Burgas Museum discovered a prehistoric mound and objects dating to the early Chalcolithic era, 10 kilometers north of the city in the "Solna Niva" area, near the Atanasovo Lake. Over 250 items have been excavated, the earliest of which are believed to have been used 6000 BC. They bear witness to agriculture, animal husbandry and salt mining, as the livelihood of the ancient inhabitants of the area. Ritual vessels of the priest-king were also discovered.

10. Tursis

Tursis is another Thracian settlement that was located west of Burgas and was destroyed at the beginning of the 2nd century BC [43] Due to active construction in later eras, the remains of the ancient settlement in the area "Sladki Kladenci" have not been preserved. However, the presence of a port, a market center and archaeological finds gave the historian Ivan Karayotov reason to locate the Port of the Thracian Kings on the territory of Burgas.
